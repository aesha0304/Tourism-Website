About Jungfraujoch

Jungfraujoch is a saddle in the Bernese Alps, connecting the two four-thousander peaks Jungfrau and MÃ¶nch. It is a popular tourist destination and is often referred to as the Top of Europe. Jungfraujoch is accessible by the Jungfrau Railway, which climbs from Kleine Scheidegg to the railway station at Jungfraujoch, making it the highest railway station in Europe.

Visitors to Jungfraujoch can experience stunning views of the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps, and enjoy activities such as visiting the Ice Palace, Snow Fun Park, and hiking in the high-altitude Alpine environment. The Sphinx Observatory, located at the top of Jungfraujoch, offers a 360-degree view of the surrounding peaks and valleys.
