About Zermatt

Zermatt is a picturesque mountain village nestled in the Swiss Alps. It offers world-class skiing, mountaineering, and breathtaking views of the iconic Matterhorn. Zermatt is car-free, ensuring clean air and a tranquil atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy the Gornergrat Railway, which provides stunning views of the surrounding peaks and glaciers. During the summer, the area is perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring the pristine alpine landscape. The Matterhorn, with its iconic pyramid shape, towers over the village and is a must-see for any visitor.

Other notable attractions in Zermatt include the Matterhorn Museum, which offers insights into the history and culture of the region, and the Glacier Paradise, the highest cable car station in Europe. Visitors can also explore the charming streets of Zermatt, filled with shops, restaurants, and traditional chalets. For adventure seekers, there are numerous trails for hiking and mountain biking, as well as opportunities for paragliding and helicopter tours.
