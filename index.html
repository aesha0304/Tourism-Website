Places to Visit

Unleash your adventurous spirit in Zermatt, home to the iconic Matterhorn; step into a fairytale in Lucerne, a lakeside medieval gem; ascend to the Top of Europe at Jungfraujoch for breathtaking views; and dive into adrenaline-pumping activities amidst stunning alpine lakes in Interlaken.

Zermatt and the Matterhorn

Nestled in the Swiss Alps, Zermatt is a picturesque mountain village offering world-class skiing and mountaineering. The Matterhorn, with its iconic pyramid shape, towers over the village and is a must-see for any visitor. Zermatt is car-free, ensuring clean air and a tranquil atmosphere. The Gornergrat Railway provides stunning views of the surrounding peaks and glaciers. Summer brings opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploring the pristine alpine landscape.

Lucerne

Lucerne, a charming city set on the shores of Lake Lucerne, is known for its preserved medieval architecture. The Chapel Bridge, a covered wooden bridge dating back to the 14th century, is a symbol of the city. The nearby Old Town boasts narrow cobblestone streets, colorful frescoed buildings, and bustling squares. Mount Pilatus and Mount Rigi offer breathtaking views and are accessible via scenic train rides and cable cars. The Swiss Transport Museum and Richard Wagner Museum add a cultural touch to your visit.

Jungfraujoch

Known as the "Top of Europe," Jungfraujoch sits at an elevation of 3,454 meters in the Bernese Alps. Visitors reach this breathtaking location via the Jungfrau Railway, which winds through tunnels in the mountains. At the summit, enjoy panoramic views of the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps. Attractions include the Ice Palace, with its intricate ice sculptures, and the Sphinx Observatory, offering a bird’s-eye view of the surrounding peaks. Jungfraujoch is a year-round destination, perfect for snow sports and high-altitude adventures.

Interlaken

Situated between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz, Interlaken is an adventure sports haven surrounded by stunning mountain scenery. The town is a gateway to the Jungfrau region and offers activities such as paragliding, skydiving, and canyoning. A boat ride on either lake provides serene views and access to picturesque villages. The Harder Kulm viewpoint, accessible by funicular, offers panoramic vistas of Interlaken and the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau peaks. With its vibrant nightlife and numerous festivals, Interlaken is as lively as it is beautiful.

Bern

Switzerland's capital, Bern, is a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its well-preserved medieval architecture. The Old Town, encircled by the Aare River, features charming arcades, fountains, and the Zytglogge clock tower with its astronomical clock. The Rose Garden offers stunning views of the city and the river below. Visit the Einstein Museum to learn about the famous physicist's time in Bern. The Federal Palace, home to Switzerland’s parliament, and the Bear Park, housing the city’s symbol, add to Bern’s unique charm.
